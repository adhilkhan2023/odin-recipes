Black Tea

Description

Black tea (also literally translated as red tea from various East Asian languages) is a type of tea that is more oxidized than oolong, yellow, white and green teas. Black tea is generally stronger in flavour than other teas. All five types are made from leaves of the shrub (or small tree) Camellia sinensis, though Camellia taliensis is also used rarely.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups water
  • 2 teaspoon tea powder
  • 1 teaspoon cane sugar optional

Steps

  1. To begin with first boil water well.
  2. Add tea powder.
  3. Boil for atleast 3 mins.
  4. The color changes to darker shade.
  5. Look at the color.
  6. Switch off.
  7. Remove from flame, close with lid and set aside for few mins for all the flavours to steep in.
  8. Now add sugar. I used cane sugar.
  9. Strain and add black tea to it.
  10. Mix well.
  11. Add to serving glass and serve Black tea hot!
